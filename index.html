Our story

We met on Christmas Eve 2007 at MudClub in Bognor Regis through a mutual friend, Jasmine. Just six months later, we moved in together in sunny Bournemouth, where we both studied at university.

While studying at Bournemouth University, we welcomed our wonderful son, Jacob, into the world. After graduating, we looked to move where Anthony got his first role as a software developer, in Oxfordshire, and we have lived here happily ever since.

In October 2021, during a trip to London, Anthony (finally) proposed to Cheryl at the top of The O2 and, as you’ve probably guessed by now, she said yes!

Although we wanted to get married, we felt that securing our own home was the priority, and in 2023 we became the proud owners of our home in the beautiful town of Chipping Norton (yes, right near Diddly Squat Farm!).

We really hope you’ll be able to join us on our special day and experience a little of the magic of Oxfordshire and the Cotswolds, which have been our home for the past 13 years.

Venue

Cogges Manor Farm is a historic manor house and farm set within seventeen acres of beautiful open space, including an adventure playground, orchard picnic areas, and a stunning walled garden.

When Jacob was younger, Cheryl would visit Cogges farm at least once a week, as Jacob loved a particularly greedy goat called Jonathon. This means we have lots of happy memories visiting Cogges, so when we discovered it was also a licensed wedding venue, we knew it would be the perfect place to celebrate our special day.

Even if you’ve never visited Cogges Manor Farm before, you may still recognise it from the ITV drama Downton Abbey, where it features as the fictional Yew Tree Farm.

Note the venue is open to the public during the day, and guests are welcome to access the farm free of charge from 9:30 am on the day of the service. There will also be plenty of time to explore the farm and gardens after the ceremony.

You can learn more about Cogges Manor Farm and the 1,000 year history of the manor house on their website.

FAQ

What time should I arrive?

Wedding guests are welcome to arrive from 12:00 pm, with the ceremony taking place between 1:00 pm and 1:30 pm. Please see the schedule for the full timings of the day.

Is there a dress code?

Smart casual or cocktail attire would be perfect. Comfortable shoes are recommended as the venue has lawns and farm paths to explore.

Can I bring children?

Children are very welcome. There is a playground and animals to see at Cogges, so there is plenty to keep younger guests entertained.

Is parking available?

Yes, free parking is available on site. See the travel section for the sat nav postcode, what3words locations, and public transport options.

Can we explore the farm on the day?

Absolutely. The farm is open to the public from 9:30 am, and wedding guests can access it free of charge. There is also time built into the schedule to explore the farm and gardens after the ceremony.

What about dietary requirements?

Please let us know of any dietary requirements when you RSVP. We also ask that any snacks brought to the venue do not contain nuts, due to a guest with a serious nut allergy.
