Our story

We met on Christmas Eve 2007 at MudClub in Bognor Regis through a mutual friend, Jasmine. Just six months later, we moved in together in sunny Bournemouth, where we both studied at university.

While studying at Bournemouth University, we welcomed our wonderful son, Jacob, into the world. After graduating, Anthony found a job in Oxfordshire, where we’ve happily lived ever since.

In 2021, during an anniversary trip to London, Anthony (finally) proposed to Cheryl at the top of The O2 and, as you’ve probably guessed by now, she said yes!

Although we wanted to get married, we felt that securing our own home was the priority, and in 2023 we became the proud owners of our home in the beautiful town of Chipping Norton (yes, right near Diddly Squat Farm!).

We really hope you’ll be able to join us on our special day and experience a little of the magic of Oxfordshire and the Cotswolds, which have been our home for the past 13 years.

Venue

Cogges Manor Farm is a historic manor house and farm set within seventeen acres of beautiful open space, including an adventure playground, orchard picnic areas, a stunning walled garden.

When Jacob was younger, Cheryl would visit Cogges farm at least once a week, as he loved a particularly greedy goat called Jonathon. This meant that we had lots of happy memories of visiting Cogges, so when we discovered it was also a licensed wedding venue, we knew it would be the perfect place to celebrate our special day.

Even if you’ve never visited Cogges Manor Farm before, you may still recognise it from the ITV drama Downton Abbey, where it featured as the fictional Yew Tree Farm.

Note the venue is open to the public during the day, and guests are welcome to access the farm free of charge from 9:30 am on the day of the service.

You can learn more about Cogges Manor Farm and the 1,000 year history of the manor house on their website.
