FAQ

What time should I arrive?

Wedding guests are welcome to arrive from 12:00 pm, with the ceremony taking place between 1:00 pm and 1:30 pm. Please see the schedule for the full timings of the day.

Is there a dress code?

Smart casual or cocktail attire would be perfect. Comfortable shoes are recommended as the venue has lawns and farm paths to explore.

Can I bring children?

Children are very welcome. There is a playground and animals to see at Cogges, so there is plenty to keep younger guests entertained.

Is parking available?

Yes, free parking is available on site, and guests are welcome to leave their cars overnight. See the travel section for the sat nav postcode, what3words locations, and public transport options.

Is there disabled access?

Cogges is a heritage site, so some areas are more accessible than others. There is a dedicated disabled car park (see the travel section), and accessible toilets are available on site. The café and gift shop are wheelchair accessible, and the ground floor of the Manor House can be accessed with a narrow wheelchair. For full details, see Cogges’s accessibility page, or email us at {{ site.email }} if you have specific requirements and we will do our best to help.

Is there onsite accommodation?

Cogges is a heritage site and does not have accommodation on site. There are plenty of hotels and B&Bs nearby. See the accommodation section for suggestions.

Can we explore the farm on the day?

Absolutely. The farm is open to the public from 9:30 am, and wedding guests can access it free of charge. There is also time built into the schedule to explore the farm and gardens after the ceremony.

What about dietary requirements?

Please let us know of any dietary requirements when you RSVP. We also ask that any snacks brought to the venue do not contain nuts, due to a guest with a serious nut allergy.
