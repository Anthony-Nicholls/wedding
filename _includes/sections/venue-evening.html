Cogges Manor Farm is a historic manor house and farm set within seventeen acres of beautiful open space, including an adventure playground, orchard picnic areas, and a stunning walled garden.

When Jacob was younger, Cheryl would visit Cogges farm at least once a week, as Jacob loved a particularly greedy goat called Jonathon. This means we have lots of happy memories visiting Cogges, so when we discovered it was also a licensed wedding venue, we knew it would be the perfect place to celebrate our special day.

Even if you’ve never visited Cogges Manor Farm before, you may still recognise it from the ITV drama Downton Abbey, where it features as the fictional Yew Tree Farm.


You can learn more about Cogges Manor Farm and the 1,000 year history of the manor house on their website.
